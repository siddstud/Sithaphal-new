Sita Mata & The Divine Fruit

During her exile in the forest, Sita Mata is said to have survived on this sweet, creamy fruit. The local people, witnessing her strength and purity, named it "Sita-phal" in her honor. The fruit's bumpy green exterior is said to resemble Sita's veil, symbolizing how true beauty and strength often lie beneath the surface.

"Just as Sita's inner strength sustained her through trials, this fruit became a symbol of resilience and divine nourishment."

Illustration of Sita Mata during her exile in the forest

The Legend

Known as the "fruit of the gods," Sithaphal's legend is deeply intertwined with the Ramayana. During her exile, Sita Mata found sustenance in this fruit, and its name "Sita-phal" became a testament to her resilience. The fruit's unique appearance - a rough exterior hiding sweet, nourishing flesh - mirrors Sita's own journey of strength and purity.

"The fruit that sustained Sita Mata in the forest now brings divine nourishment to your table."
